Tesla Dojo Supercomputer

The custom-built AI training supercomputer powering FSD and Optimus.

Dojo Exaflop Compute Explained:

Dojo is Tesla's in-house supercomputer designed specifically for training massive neural networks on vast amounts of video data from the Tesla fleet. It uses custom D1/AI5 chips optimized for sparse tensor operations and video workloads, achieving **1.1+ exaflops** of BF16 compute in a single ExaPOD configuration (120 trays with over 1 million cores).

An **exaflop** equals 1 quintillion (10^18) floating-point operations per second — roughly equivalent to millions of high-end GPUs working together. Dojo's architecture delivers this at high efficiency with massive memory bandwidth (1.2+ exabytes/second) and low-latency interconnects, allowing Tesla to train FSD and Optimus models 4x faster than traditional GPU clusters while using less power.

Dojo is critical for accelerating progress toward unsupervised autonomy and humanoid robotics.
